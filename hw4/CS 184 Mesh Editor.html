Overview

In this project, I have implemented physical simulation of cloth under basic gravity and interactions with itself and other solid objects. The simulation utilizes Verlet Intergration for position updates and spacial hashing for accelerated self collision check. The project also includes a real-time shader that supports diffuse and Bling-Phong shading, bump, displacement, and texture mapping, and mirror environment mapping.

Describe the effects of changing the spring constant ks; how does the cloth behave from start to rest with a very low ks? A high ks?

With a high ks, the cloth is modeled to be more elastic and the simulation reflects this increasing elasticity. When the cloth reaches the rest state, the drip of the cloth is smaller, and when it's falling, a higher ks makes the creases disappears faster. On the opposite a small ks causes a larger drip, and there are more little swings and more creses on the cloth when the cloth is falling.

What about for density?

For both increasing and decreasing, changing density will not impact how fast the cloth is falling, which follows real world physics. Opposite to Ks, increase density will cause the drip between the two pinned point larger as the cloth is getting heavier whereas decreasing density will cause smaller drip. One thing different from the effect caused by changing the Ks, large density will not cause more creases as low Ks do, but actually less creases since the cloth is more dragged downward.

What about for damping?

Damping is perhaps impacting the most to the cloth's motion when it's falling. If we set damping to very low, the cloth will sway back and forth and we can see a lot of creases appear on the cloth, and if we set damping to zero, the cloth will sway forever. On the contrary, set a high damping lead to a much slower, less "aggresive" falling, there is nearly no signs of creases when falling, and the cloth rest immediately when the falling stops.

Part 3: Handling collisions with other objects

The established pointmass and spring system now need to account for collisions with other objects. In this part, we will handle collisions with planes and spheres specifically. For planes, we check at each iteration whether a pointmass is going to cross the plane, and if so, we pushed its position back on the plane. Similarly for spheres, we check whether a pointmass is going to enter into the sphere and if so, we puhsed its position back to the surface of the sphere.

With other variables kept the same, increasing the density of the cloth makes the cloth less spread out when it folds on itself because the original spring force won't push away the cloth as easy as when the cloth is lighter in the default configuration. Explain in your own words what is a shader program and how vertex and fragment shaders work together to create lighting and material effects.

A shadre program includes a vertex shader and a fragment shader. The whole program runs parallel on the GPU and takes in the verticies geometry of the objects in the scene and outputs the pixel colors displayed on the screen. Specifically, the vertex shader performs transformations on input verticies' position and output both world space vertex positions and screen space vertex position. The latter will be taken for rasterization, and the prior will be taken by the fragment shader to calculate the color.

Extra Credit (optional)

If you implemented any additional technical features for the cloth simulation, clearly describe what you did and provide screenshots that illustrate your work. If it is an improvement compared to something already existing on the cloth simulation, compare and contrast them both in words and in images.

I added wind forces for the cloth simulation. In this simulation, there is a horizontal wind blowing to the negative x direction -- left side of the screen -- pointing at the origin of the sphere in the scene. To implement this, I added a horizontal acceleration vector upon the given gravity acceleration, then I used Newton's second law to apply this wind acceleration on pointmasses. However, different points receives different wind force according to their normal vector and position. Pointmasses that are more verticle will receive more wind force and Pointmasses closer to (0.2, 0.5) on the y-z plane(the origin of the sphere) will receive more wind force. The wind is also made random within a certain scale. The following images shows the simulation of the sphere scene. We can observe that during falling, since the cloth is horizontal, horizontal wind force won't act on the cloth, and after the cloth bends, we can see the cloth is gradually swaying to the left and eventually fall off. Changing the direction of the wind and simulate the selfCollision scene, we can see that the cloth does not vertically folded on itself but is blown to the left. Furthermore, by lowering the plane and let the cloth falls off, we can notice the creaes created by the wind and the cloth is slightly bended in the air. The creases also shows the falloff of the wind force and where the wind centers at.
